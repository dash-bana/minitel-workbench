# Credits

Minitel Workbench is a community project. It exists because other people kept the
Minitel alive first — this list acknowledges them, in no order of importance.

- **Francesco Sblendorio** — for **Retrocampus**, one of the most active and
  generous corners of the living Minitel world (free to access; AI for Patreon
  supporters).
- **The MiniPavi maintainer(s)** — for the gateway and live directory that put
  hundreds of services one code away.
- **pR-0000** — for the *Minitel RS232/USB Telnet Interface*, the community bridge
  that first got a real terminal back online and directly inspired Workbench's
  own bridge.
- **The people who build the USB↔DIN adapters and refurbish terminals** — without
  the cable, none of the direct-connection features would exist.
- **Le Musée du Minitel** and **Musée Minitel Alcatel** — for preserving the
  hardware, documents, and history, and for making them public.
- **Everyone on the Musée du Minitel forum** and the wider preservation community
  — for the restoration notes, Videotex art, and patient answers.

If you contributed and aren't listed, that's an oversight worth a pull request —
this file is meant to grow.

## How Workbench relates to them

Workbench's job is to send people **toward** these projects, not to replace or
absorb them. The service directory features Retrocampus and MiniPavi; the
Resources view links out to the museums and archives; and the default posture for
any borrowed material is *link and attribute* until an author says otherwise
(see the [Constitution](CONSTITUTION.md), rules VIII and X).
